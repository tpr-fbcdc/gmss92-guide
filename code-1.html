Technical Summary

The 1-Flash code indicates the control board is not detecting a flame signal. This typically means the furnace attempts to ignite but the flame dies within 2-5 seconds, or no ignition occurs at all.

Safety First: If you smell gas, turn off the gas supply immediately and ventilate the area before proceeding.

Symptoms

  • Furnace lights for 2-5 seconds, then flame dies
  • Igniter glows but no flame appears
  • Repeated ignition attempts followed by lockout
  • Gas odor during failed ignition cycles

Diagnostic Steps

1

Inspect the Hot Surface Igniter

Check the igniter for visible cracks, white "hot spots," or thinning. A damaged igniter will glow weakly or unevenly.

2

Clean the Flame Sensor

⚠️ POWER OFF FIRST — The flame sensor sits near the high-voltage igniter. Remove the flame sensor (single screw mount). Clean the metal rod with a light abrasive pad (Scotch-Brite or fine sandpaper) to remove oxidation buildup.

3

Check Flame Sensor Microamp Reading

With the furnace running, measure DC microamps between the flame sensor wire and ground. Normal reading: 1.5-6 microamps. Below 1 microamp indicates a dirty or failing sensor.

4

Verify Gas Pressure

Check manifold gas pressure at the test port. Should be 3.2-3.8" WC for natural gas. Low pressure prevents proper flame establishment.

5

Inspect Ground Connection

Ensure the furnace has a proper ground. A poor ground can cause erratic flame sensing. Check the ground wire from the control board to the furnace cabinet.

Common Causes & Remediation

Dirty Flame Sensor (Most Common)
Oxidation on the sensor rod prevents accurate flame detection.
Fix: Clean with abrasive pad or replace sensor (Part #0130F00010)
Cracked or Weak Igniter
Igniter doesn't reach proper temperature to ignite gas.
Fix: Replace Hot Surface Igniter
Low Gas Pressure
Insufficient pressure for stable flame.
Fix: Check gas valve, regulator, and supply line
Poor Grounding
Flame sensing circuit requires proper ground reference.
Fix: Clean and tighten ground connections
